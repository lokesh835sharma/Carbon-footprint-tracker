The Science of Carbon Footprints

Understanding your impact is the first step to reducing it.

1. The Impact of Transportation

Transportation accounts for over one-quarter of greenhouse gas emissions. A typical passenger vehicle emits about 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide per year. By switching just one car trip a week to public transit, biking, or walking, you can significantly shrink your footprint.

Fact: Riding a bus reduces CO2 emissions by up to 80% compared to driving alone.

2. The Food Print

What you eat matters. The production of meat, particularly beef, requires massive amounts of land, water, and energy, releasing significant methane—a greenhouse gas much more potent than CO2.

Fact: A plant-based diet can reduce your food-related carbon footprint by up to 73%.

3. Energy at Home

Residential energy use is a major contributor to global emissions. Simple habits like turning off lights, unplugging "vampire" electronics, and using energy-efficient appliances add up quickly.

Fact: Heating and cooling account for nearly half of a typical home's energy use.
